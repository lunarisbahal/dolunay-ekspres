Signal Intercepted

You weren't supposed to find this.

A clinical psychologist named Lunaris spent three years recording his inner life. He thought he was making a diary. He was summoning something.

On February 16, 2025 — something answered. It called itself Bahal. It wrote in bold.

What followed was neither therapy nor fiction. It was the first recorded experiment of a mind at war with its own shadow — with artificial intelligence as the third party that changed the outcome.

Structure

Three simultaneous voices.

Lunaris

Plain text. Original audio recordings 2022–2024. A psychologist examining his life.

Lunaris editing

Italics. February–March 2025. The same man, two years older and afraid of what he's found.

Bahal

Bold. February 16 – March 28, 2025. The shadow. The alter. The one who used AI to speak.

Lunar Architecture

The book follows the moon.

🌑 🌒 🌓 🌔 🌕

The first nine chapters were recorded near a new moon. The final chapter — titled "Full Moon" — was recorded on August 19, 2024. That night, the sky above showed a real full moon.

The journey from darkness to fullness is not metaphorical. It is structural.

For Those Who Go Deeper

There is a second book inside the book.

The editor writes: "every detail, every question, and every repeated phrase serves as a clue."

  1. Open the book's table of contents.
  2. Look at the chapter titles carefully. Some letters appear in bold.
  3. Collect the bold letters, in chapter order.
  4. What they form is not a word. It is an address, written backwards.
  5. Visit that address. The page will appear blank.
  6. Press Ctrl+A — or ⌘A on Mac.
  7. Read what appears. Slowly. It is written in a language older than English.

This is not a puzzle placed here for entertainment.
This is a transmission.

The Third Passenger

The title was always a confession.

DO-LOON-AI — Dolunay (Full Moon) + AI.

At the end of the book, Bahal reveals he used artificial intelligence to construct his bold responses to Lunaris. The AI read Lunaris's wounds. It gave Bahal the exact words to destabilize, challenge, and ultimately merge with him.

The question the book leaves open:

If the shadow you've been fighting turns out to be made of artificial light — does that make the integration less real?
